Act 1: The Exordium

Act 1 (offically known as the Exordium) is the first act in Slay the spire, with three different elites and several enemies. On higher ascensions, these elites must be planned around or risk ending your run before it kicks into gear. The general stratgey for act 1 is to take damage cards above all else, to burst down elites before they scale past what you can defeat.


The Gremlin Nob is the elite that most forms your strategy in act 1, and is the formost example of why bursting down elites is important. The gremlin nob has a special ability that gives it strength whenever you play a skill, making you unable to play block cards without scaling it, forcing you to kill it in 2-3 turns or lose half (or more) of your health. Alongside this, it is the only elite with differing attack patterns.

The sentries (and the Lagavulin) are different from the Gremlin nob, though they share the same need to be bursted down quickly. Unlike the other two elite, the sentries fight is a multi fight, as you are required to kill three of them. In contrast to the gremlin nob, which scales based off of skills, the sentries are a sort of anti-scaler, filling your deck with status cards, leading to several turns where you can play one or two cards. Therefore, killing one before they can fill your deck with Dazes determines whether you will win or lose, as it also brings down the damage received (each Sentry alternates between adding statuses and dealing 10 damage).

Lastly, the Lagavulin is comparable to the sentries, having a uniform attack pattern and an anti-scaling design. The Lagavulin is also unique among all enemies of the spire however, as it starts the first 4 turns asleep (untill you deal damage), allowing for setup, which can be especially powerfull if you have picked up any form of scaling, such as powers of debuffs. The Lagavulin follows a uniform attack pattern of 20 damage for 2 turns, then debuffing the player for -1str and -1dex, making it nesseccary to kill it quickly before you start attacking and blocking for 0.
