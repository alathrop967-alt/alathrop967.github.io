Bosses



















Act 1 bosses are a damage race, similar to Act 1 elites. The slime boss splits into two smaller slimes when below 50% hp (and splits again when at 50% for the smaller slimes), so getting a good split is the most important matter. The guardian is a consistancy test, with alternating damage, block, and damage with thorns, forcing you to have consistant ways to block and do lots of damage to force him to curl up. The hexa-ghost is a damage test; it buffs itself on turn one, does scaling damage (scaling based off your current health), adds burns to your hand, and does a massive attack and upgrades all your statuses on turn 6.


Act 2 bosses are much more based on scaling then front loaded damage, with the Collector and Bronze Automaton having specific turns you need to plan around (massive debuff and massive damage respectively). The Champ is all but trivialized with the right scaling (such as a demon form or lots of focus), as before he reaches less then 50% health, his move pattern is not very threatening. The collector and the Bronze Automaton both summon minions, though the collector can do it multiple times compared to the Automaton only doing it on turn 1, though the Automaton's minions steal your rarest card, making them more dangerous then the collector's.

Act 3 bosses are all very different from each other, requring you to build around them as early as you can (you can see what current boss you are facing). The awakened one has two forms and two cultist's helping him, gaining strength when you play a power in his first form, and transforming into a higher damaging second form that can add statuses into your deck when you kill its first form. The Timer Eater ends your turn and gains strength when you play 12 cards, punishing low value cards and disallowing infinites. Alongside this, the Timer Eater also heals to 50% once he drops below 50% one time. Lastly, Donu and Deca is the only true boss multifight, as all the others only have minions rather the another enemy. When both are alive, you will always be receiving damage, as Deca alternates between adding statuses to your deck and damage, and Donu alternates between buffing both enemies and damage. Because of this, you will almost always want to burst down Donu as fast as possible to stop the strength scaling.
