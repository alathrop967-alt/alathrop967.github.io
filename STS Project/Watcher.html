The Watcher

Stance Dancing:

The most basic, yet overpowered, strategy that the Watcher posses. The watcher has 3 stances, but for the sake of this section, we will only be covering the two basic ones: Wrath, which doubles the damage you deal and take, and calm, which gives you two energy when you leave it. First off, the watcher has a card, flurry of blows, that gets returned to your hand whenever you change stances. Costing 0 and dealing 4 damage, it is a useful bit of chip damage, but in the right strategy, can easily do upwards of 20-40 damage in a single turn. This is enabled mainly through rushdown, a power that draws two cards when you enter wrath, which when combined with leaving calm giving you energy, can easily lead to incredibly long and powerful turns.

Retain:

Arguably the Watcher's weakest strategy, her multide of retain cards and cards that buff retained cards are nontheless powerful and fun. The core card of this strategy is Establishment, a power that makes cards cost less when they are retained. Establishment, combined with something like battle hymn, which creates a retaining attack and master reality, which upgrades created cards, can easily lead to a steamroll of longer fights (mainly boss fights), thoguh strugglign against faster paced hallway fights, and especially elite fights.

Divinity:

Divinity is the final and most powerful stance that the watcher has, giving you 3 energy and making you deal triple damage. While wrath and calm can be entered easily by and wrath or calm card, Divinity requires 10 mantra first (with the execption of blasphemy, which puts you in wrath but kills you next turn). The watcher has 4 cardsm (3 powers, 1 skill) and 1 relic that can give you mantra, making it a hard strategy to spec into. If you onyl have one of these cards, they are an active detriment, as you will likley never enter divinity in all but the longest boss fights. Therefore, this strategy is best when you have either gotten Darumama (the relic) or have transoformed a card that gave you mantra generation, as it means that the next mantra card added makes your deck vastly more powerful but letting you consistantly enter divintiy.
